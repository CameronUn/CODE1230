Urban Transformations





Definition of an Urban Transformation

An urban design project designed by a group of people to achieve multiple tasks. It is no bigger than the city itself,
nor smaller than a single building. Itâ€™s aim is to provide a functional and efficient landscape to provide a space for industry
and public to utilise and achieve a collection of varied outcomes. The two transformations; Barangaroo and Green Square present two varied examples
of modern urban transformations, where an unused or inefficiently used space is transformed into an urban landmark, with public and private facilities.


Why do the Examples Meet the Definition

They represent a complete alteration of a space no bigger than the city or a single building and provide a landscape that is used by industry
and the public. Barangaroo is a space that was transformed completely, a once inefficient use of space alongside a prime location, it is a public space
that is inhabited with industry, public space like walkways, facilities, parklands, industry such as shops and office buildings, and a soon to be casino.

Green Square Plaza is a complete transformation to make the suburb a community hub, creating a space full of public facilities to increase
the awareness and activity in an almost forgotten suburb. Both urban transformations intend to create a space to improve social behavior and movement
efficiency, and therefore achieving a display of various outcomes.
